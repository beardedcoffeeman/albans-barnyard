# Your New Alban's Barnyard Website

Hi Simon & Lesley! Here's a guide to your new website and what's changed.

---

## What's New

### A Modern, Premium Design
The new site is built to feel like a luxury brand, inspired by sites like Daylesford. It's fast, looks beautiful on phones and computers, and is designed to make people want to book.

### Every Season Changes Automatically
The website detects the time of year and updates the homepage banner to match:
- **Spring** - Promotes the Lambcam and lambing stays
- **Summer** - Highlights the farm, honey harvest, and countryside
- **Autumn** - Features meat boxes and cosy autumn breaks
- **Winter** - Focuses on the wood stove and winter escapes

### The Lambcam
Both cameras are on one page now with a button to switch between them, or view both side-by-side. There's a "LIVE" indicator and it loads much faster than before.

### Booking System
Visitors can see which dates are available on a calendar and request a booking. You get notified and can approve or decline each request. No booking is confirmed without your say-so.

### Guest Book
The handwritten guest book PDFs are embedded on the site so visitors can browse through real entries, drawings, and messages from past guests. Much more authentic than typed reviews.

### Farm Shop
Sheepskins, honey, and meat boxes each have their own section with photos and descriptions. People can enquire to order directly.

### Found on Google
The site has been built with search engines in mind. We've included all the right keywords so that people searching for things like "holiday cottage Kent" or "farm stay near London" can find you. There's also a sitemap and structured data that helps Google understand what the site is about.

## How to Edit the Website

Go to: **[your website address]/admin**

Password: **albans2026**

You'll see five tabs:

### 1. Assistant
This is like having a helper. Type what you want in plain English and it will do it for you. For example:
- "Make the whole of June available at £170 per night"
- "Show me pending bookings"
- "Turn off the lambcam for the summer"

*(Note: This needs an API key to be set up. Tom will sort this for you.)*

### 2. Pages
This is where you edit the words and images on each page. Click any section to expand it. You'll see a preview on the left and the editable fields on the right. Change the text, hit Save, and it updates the website.

### 3. Media
Upload new photos or videos here. You can also see all the existing images on the site. Click the copy button on any image to copy its path, which you can paste into a page section's image field.

### 4. Bookings
See all booking requests here. Each one shows the guest's name, dates, number of guests, and any message. Click Approve or Decline.

### 5. Analytics
See how many people are visiting the site, which pages are most popular, and where visitors are coming from.
